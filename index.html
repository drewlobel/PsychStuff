1. Ethical Guidelines

What are the four main ethical standards for human research?
1. Informed Consent: Info on purpose, risks, and right to withdraw[cite: 29, 30].
2. Confidentiality: Right to privacy[cite: 38].
3. Deception: Only if no alternative; must debrief[cite: 42, 45].
4. Free of Harm: Protection from physical/psychological danger[cite: 46].

2. The Neuron & Neural Impulse

Building Blocks of the Brain

Neurons are individual nerve cells that transmit messages[cite: 68, 69].

PartFunction
DendritesShort fibers that collect messages from other cells[cite: 82, 86].
Soma (Cell Body)Carries out basic cell functions; contains the nucleus[cite: 87, 91].
AxonLong fiber through which messages leave the neuron[cite: 92, 96].
Myelin SheathFatty white substance that speeds up impulses[cite: 98, 106].
What is the difference between Resting and Action Potentials?
Resting Potential: Cell is more negative inside; membrane is semi-permeable (Polarized)[cite: 152, 153].
Action Potential: Cell is stimulated/fired; more positive ions flow in (Depolarized)[cite: 154, 163].

3. Divisions of the Nervous System

How do the Sympathetic and Parasympathetic systems differ?
Sympathetic: Activation/arousal; "Fight or Flight" (pounding heart, quick breathing)[cite: 208, 213, 216].
Parasympathetic: Slows body down; maintains Homeostasis (equilibrium)[cite: 218, 220, 223].

4. Brain Regions & Lobes

What are the three major regions of the brain?
1. Hindbrain: Primitive level; supports vital life functions (Medulla, Pons, Cerebellum)[cite: 237, 246].
2. Midbrain: Sensory/motor clusters; auditory and visual reflexes[cite: 265, 277].
3. Forebrain: Most advanced portion; includes Thalamus and Hypothalamus[cite: 281, 282, 287].
Name the four lobes of the Cerebral Cortex and their roles.
  • Frontal: Planning, decision making, motor control (Broca's area)[cite: 8].
  • Parietal: Touch, temperature, body sense (Somatosensory cortex)[cite: 8].
  • Temporal: Hearing, speech comprehension (Wernicke's area)[cite: 8].
  • Occipital: Vision and sight processing[cite: 9].
